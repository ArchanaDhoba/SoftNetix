Nutrition Tips

What is Nutrition?

"the process of taking food into the body and absorbing the nutrients in those foods."

Basically, we need nutrients from food to survive and thrive. While food is certainly meant to be enjoyed, it's about more than just taste and pleasure. Food provides the nutrients that our body needs for everything it does, including digestion, breathing, thinking, and providing energy for all movement

"Drinking enough water every day is good for overall health and can help manage body weight, according to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC)Trusted Source.

If someone does not like plain water, they can add some citrus slices and mint leaves to increase the appeal, or drink herbal teas."

Drinking water can prevent dehydration, which can be a particular risk for older adultsTrusted Source.

Eat your greens


Dark green leafy vegetables are a great source of nutrition, according to the Department of Agriculture (USDA).

Leafy greens are rich in vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants.

The USDA suggest that folate in leafy greens may help protect against cancer, while vitamin K helps prevent osteoporosis.

Poha and Indian Breakfast


Poha, a popular Indian breakfast dish made from flattened rice, has many health benefits that can make it a good choice for a healthy start to the day:

Low in caloriesPoha is a good option for those looking to manage their weight or reduce calorie intake. A bowl of poha can have as little as 180 calories.

Good probioticPoha can help develop a healthy gut, which can prevent unintended weight gain.

Idli has many health benefits, including:


Weight loss: Idli is low in calories and fat, and high in protein and fiber, which can help you feel full and control hunger.

Blood sugar regulation: Idli's protein content can help control blood sugar levels.

Digestion: Idli is a fermented food that contains gut bacteria and probiotics, which can help improve digestion and boost immunity.
